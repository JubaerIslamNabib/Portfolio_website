PROJECTS

AIRGRIP - PNEUMATIC SOFT GRIPPER

Pneumatic soft robots, characterized by their lightweight design, high efficiency, and environmental adaptability, hold a key position in this domain. Their gentle and compliant nature makes them suitable for medical applications like mimicking biological limbs ,movement or creating comfortable and sustainable prosthetics for handicapped people. In this project , We made a gripper with the intention of mimicking our hand movements. As soon as the finger starts to bent , the resistance value begins to increase which can be found due to input of the flex sensor. The motor starts when this resistance passes the threshold. Thus the fingers of the gripper bend and it can even hold things of smaller weight . We made this project with the hope that it can be used as prosthetics , robotic surgery.

Modelling Drug Particle Transport and Deposition in the Tracheal Airway

Inhaled medications provide targeted respiratory treatment with minimal side effects, but their effectiveness depends on drug particle deposition. Computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulates airflow and particle trajectories, optimizing inhaler designs and patient-specific therapies for conditions like asthma and COPD. In our lab, we simulated inhaled drug delivery using a combination of Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) and physical airway models. This allowed us to replicate airflow and particle deposition patterns, providing insights into drug dispersion in the respiratory system.
